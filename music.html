Frettchen, 2018-2019

Frettchen was a music project during college that included myself on keys, Jack Schrott on guitar and vocals, Stirling Rutty on bass, and Andrew Eaton on drums. We wrote quite a few songs together and played at lots of house parties and small shows on campus (including an ill-fated accidental invite to the admitted students day dinner. Think loud rock music in the corner while people and their families are trying to eat). Recording was done mostly in Colorado College's music studio in Packard Hall. We recorded 7 or 8 songs in the spring of my Senior Year. I did the mixing and mastering on the tracks, 2 of which we put up on Spotify. All the songs were co-written and co-produced by the band. Our friend Carson Fritz did the album art. A few more songs that never made it to Spotify are uploaded as well. These songs will always bring back fond memories.

Jack brought Vain to the band pretty much finished, we just added our parts. It became one of our favorites.

Pick a Song, John was a classic to open or close with. During the breakdown near the end, Andrew would sometimes thrash 1/8th notes on the snare during the build long enough to keep the whole band on their toes.

Ketching Up

This song never made to Spotify. The first part of the song was wild and a good choice for inciting chaos on the dance floor. Stirling had some particularly good bass licks in this one. Despite spending 16 hours over 4 do-overs trying to mix it, I failed to tame the wild recording, but this was my best attempt.

Solo Music

I've played piano since I was around 10 years old. I started off learning songs I liked from youtube tutorials, and eventually took lessons in highschool. In college, I met a few really awesome musicians that inspired me to learn theory and practice more seriously. I sang in CC's Room 46 A Capella group for two years, and studied jazz piano and music theory with Steve Barta for three. I also played piano in CC's jazz big band my senior year.

Most of my time playing is spent just noodling and improvising as a way to wind down. The following are some of my favorite recordings I have kicking around. I also dropped in some of my favorite voice memos, though these are mostly unfinished songs with a good bit of starting/stopping.
